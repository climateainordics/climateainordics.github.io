The Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences (SLU) is inviting applications for a tenure-track Assistant Professor position in Data-Driven Evolution and Biodiversity. This high-profile role is part of the SciLifeLab and Wallenberg National Program for Data-Driven Life Science (DDLS), a major 3.3 billion SEK initiative. The successful candidate will join the program as a "DDLS Fellow," becoming part of a prestigious national network of next-generation life scientists.

The position offers significant flexibility, allowing the candidate to choose a host department from various research environments in Uppsala or Umeå. Applicants must demonstrate a strong computational focus, specifically in developing machine learning or AI methods for biological data. The research area covers massive data streams, including genomic sequences, continuous wild audio/video recordings, and remote sensing.

A substantial recruitment package of 17 MSEK is provided over five years to cover the Fellow’s salary and funding for PhD students and postdocs. Eligible candidates must hold a PhD, ideally awarded within seven years of the application deadline. Primary duties include establishing an independent research group, securing external funding, and publishing high-impact scientific articles. The role also involves teaching at various levels and supervising postgraduate students within the DDLS research school.

While the primary working language is English, the university provides support for non-Swedish speakers to learn Swedish within four years. Candidates will have access to world-class national infrastructures, including SciLifeLab’s platforms for genomics, imaging, and high-performance computing. SLU provides unique long-term datasets from national environmental monitoring programs and field research stations to support automated analysis.

The employment follows a tenure-track model, offering a clear path to promotion to Senior Lecturer following a successful evaluation. Benefits include Swedish family healthcare, generous parental leave, and access to a high-quality, no-cost educational system. Applications are evaluated based on research expertise and the applicant's potential to lead the future of data-driven life science.

**Deadline:** April 17th, 2026
